Write

Measured Voice is designed to help you and your team write great social media messages. Nothing is more important on social media than what you say and how you say it.

Ideas

Every message starts out as an idea. Every Measured Voice account lets you quickly jot down ideas for messages and come back to work on them later.

People

Every Measured Voice account features unlimited users which means you can work with anyone on your team to gather ideas and write the best messages for your audience. You don’t have to share your Twitter password or make them admins of your Facebook page.

Guidelines

A short set of guidelines is a near perfect tool to develop and manage a brand voice. We let you create guidelines to help everyone understand what your organization talks about and how it sounds.

Workflow

We let you assign editor and contributor roles to the people you work with. Contributors can write as many messages as they want, but an editor needs to approve them before they go out.

Images

Images, whether photographs or infographics, are now an essential part of any social media content strategy. Every Measured Voice account lets you upload and post images to Facebook and Twitter.

Schedule

We believe in strategic communication, so we let you plan your messages to go out in the future. If you know what you need to say, and when you need to say it, you shouldn’t have to set an alarm to remind you – Measured Voice will post your messages when they need to go out. That way, you can focus on other things in real time (or take a nap).

Measure

We count what matters. For each post you send, we count how many people it reached, how much it was shared, and how many people liked it. This message-focused approach to metrics helps you optimize your messaging to reach the most people and have the greatest impact.

Dashboards throughout Measured Voice show you how you’re doing at a glance, but you can drill down and see how each message performed, compare messages to one another, and even export your metrics to Excel.
